1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the step-by-step process for executing monthly payroll to ensure accurate, timely, and compliant payment of employee compensation. This SOP ensures consistency across all payroll cycles and compliance with federal and state tax regulations.

2. Scope

This procedure applies to:

  • All full-time and part-time employees (W-2)
  • All company locations within the United States
  • Regular monthly payroll cycles (not applicable to off-cycle or contractor payments)

5. Procedure Steps

Phase 1: Data Collection (Day 1-5)

1 Collect Timesheet Data Payroll Specialist
  • Export approved timesheets from the time tracking system (Workday/ADP)
  • Verify all employees have submitted timesheets for the pay period
  • Flag any missing or incomplete submissions
  • Send reminder notifications to managers with pending approvals
2 Gather HR Updates HR Manager
  • Compile list of new hires with start dates within pay period
  • Document terminations and final pay calculations
  • Record salary changes, promotions, or department transfers
  • Update benefit enrollment changes (401(k), health insurance)
3 Collect Variable Pay Data Payroll Specialist
  • Gather commission reports from Sales department
  • Collect bonus authorization forms
  • Document overtime hours requiring approval
  • Record any reimbursements or expense payments

Phase 2: Payroll Processing (Day 6-10)

4 Enter Payroll Data Payroll Specialist
  • Input regular hours, overtime, and PTO used
  • Enter variable compensation (commissions, bonuses)
  • Apply salary adjustments and new hire rates
  • Process garnishments and court-ordered deductions
Warning: Double-check all manual entries. Data entry errors are the leading cause of payroll discrepancies.
5 Calculate Deductions Payroll Specialist
  • Verify federal income tax withholding based on W-4
  • Calculate state and local tax withholdings
  • Process 401(k) contributions (employee + employer match)
  • Deduct health insurance, HSA, and FSA contributions
  • Apply FICA taxes (Social Security 6.2%, Medicare 1.45%)
6 Run Preliminary Payroll Payroll Specialist
  • Execute payroll calculation in preview mode
  • Generate preliminary payroll register
  • Create departmental cost allocation reports
  • Export data for review without committing transactions

Phase 3: Review and Validation (Day 11-15)

7 Perform Quality Checks Payroll Specialist

Complete the following validation checklist:

Compare total payroll to previous month (variance < 5%)
Verify headcount matches HR records
Confirm all new hires are included with correct rates
Validate terminated employees are excluded or have final pay
Check tax calculations against published tables
Verify 401(k) contributions do not exceed annual limits
Confirm garnishment deductions are applied correctly
8 Resolve Discrepancies Payroll Manager
  • Investigate any variances flagged during quality checks
  • Contact department managers for timesheet clarifications
  • Coordinate with HR for personnel data corrections
  • Document all adjustments with justification
Note: All discrepancies over $500 must be documented in the Payroll Exception Log and require manager approval.

Phase 4: Approval and Submission (Day 16-20)

9 Manager Review and Approval Payroll Manager
  • Review final payroll register and summary reports
  • Verify all exceptions have been addressed
  • Compare to budget allocations by department
  • Sign off on payroll batch in the system
10 Finance Approval (if applicable) Finance Director
  • Required for payroll totals exceeding $500,000
  • Review funding availability in payroll account
  • Approve wire transfer or ACH batch
  • Provide final authorization in system
11 Submit Payroll for Processing Payroll Manager
  • Commit payroll batch to payment processor
  • Initiate ACH direct deposit file transmission
  • Queue check printing for non-ACH employees
  • Submit tax payment files to federal and state agencies
Critical: Submission must occur by 3:00 PM EST on Day 20 to ensure on-time payment processing.

Phase 5: Post-Processing (Day 21-25)

12 Verify Payment Transmission Payroll Specialist
  • Confirm ACH file acceptance by bank
  • Verify tax payments are scheduled correctly
  • Ensure check stock is available for printing
  • Monitor for any rejected transactions
13 Generate Reports and Archive Payroll Specialist
  • Create final payroll register (PDF and Excel)
  • Generate general ledger journal entries
  • Produce tax liability reports (941, state filings)
  • Archive all supporting documentation
  • Update payroll calendar for next cycle

6. Exception Handling

Exception Type Resolution Process Escalation
Missing timesheet Contact employee and manager; use previous period average if unresolved HR Manager if > 2 days late
Overpayment discovered Document error, calculate recovery amount, notify employee, set up repayment plan Payroll Manager for amounts > $500
Underpayment discovered Process off-cycle payment within 48 hours; document root cause Immediate correction required
Bank rejection Verify banking details, resubmit or issue manual check Finance Director if recurring
Tax calculation error Recalculate, file amended returns if necessary Finance Director and legal review

7. Compliance Requirements

  • FLSA Compliance: Ensure proper overtime calculations for non-exempt employees
  • Tax Deposits: Federal tax deposits due by the 15th of the following month
  • Form 941: Quarterly federal tax return due by the last day of the month following the quarter
  • W-2 Distribution: Annual W-2 forms must be distributed by January 31st
  • Record Retention: Maintain payroll records for minimum 7 years
