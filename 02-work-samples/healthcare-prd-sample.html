1. Product Vision

Vision Statement

Empower healthcare providers to deliver proactive, personalized care by enabling continuous remote monitoring of chronic disease patients - improving outcomes while creating sustainable revenue through RPM billing.

Problem Statement

Chronic disease patients (hypertension, diabetes, COPD) require ongoing monitoring, but traditional care models only capture health data during infrequent office visits. This leads to missed early warning signs, preventable hospitalizations, and reactive rather than proactive care. Additionally, providers struggle to capture RPM billing revenue due to manual tracking requirements.

Solution

A comprehensive B2B platform that connects healthcare providers with their patients through FDA-cleared Bluetooth medical devices, enabling real-time vitals monitoring, intelligent alerting, clinical review workflows, and automated billing - all within a HIPAA-compliant environment.

Key Value Propositions

  • For Super Admin: Multi-provider management, device inventory, request handling, activity logs
  • For Providers/Hospitals: Patient management, care team coordination, billing eligibility tracking
  • For Clinical Staff: Real-time vitals dashboard, alerts, patient profiles, medical history
  • For Patients: Simple device pairing, automatic data sync, care team visibility

3. Feature Overview

Platform Navigation Structure

The platform consists of seven main modules accessible via top navigation:

  • Dashboard - Overview stats and request management
  • Device Inventory - Device tracking and allocation
  • Providers - Healthcare provider/hospital management
  • PDC Staff - Internal staff management
  • PDC Nursing - Nursing staff workflows and requests
  • PDC Billing - Billing eligibility and reporting
  • Activity Logs - Audit trail and system logs

3.1 Super Admin Dashboard

Centralized command center for platform administrators to monitor all providers, patients, and requests.

Dashboard Stats Cards:
  • Total Providers: Active healthcare providers/hospitals (weekly change indicator)
  • Inactive Providers: Providers with suspended access
  • Total POP: Primary Ordering Providers count
  • Total Patients: All enrolled patients across providers
  • Unallotted Devices: Available devices vs total inventory (e.g., 50/500)
  • Device Allocation Requests: Pending device requests from providers
  • PDC Billing Requests: Pending billing-related requests
  • PDC Nursing Requests: Pending nursing support requests
Request Management Table:
  • Columns: S.No, Provider Name, Request Type, Patient ID, Dates, Status, Action
  • Request Types: Device Allocation, Support, PDC Nursing (Enable/Disable), PDC Billing (Enable/Disable)
  • Status tracking: Pending, Approved, Rejected
Analytics Charts:
  • Patients Onboarded (weekly/monthly trend)
  • Requests volume (weekly/monthly trend)

3.2 Device Inventory Management

Complete lifecycle management of RPM devices from procurement to patient assignment.

Device Information Modal:
  • Device ID: Unique identifier (e.g., TMB-2092-G)
  • Device Type: RPM Device, BPM Device, etc.
  • Battery Level: Percentage indicator (e.g., 100%)
  • Signal Strength: Connectivity indicator (e.g., 25%)
  • Device Status: Active, Inactive, Unassigned
  • Network Identifiers: ICCID, IMSI, IMEI numbers
  • Latest Reading: Most recent measurement with timestamp
  • Assigned Patient: Patient name, age, gender, location, last active time
Device Allocation Workflow:
  • Provider submits device allocation request with Patient ID
  • Super Admin reviews and approves/rejects
  • Device automatically linked to patient profile

3.3 Provider Management

Multi-tenant architecture supporting multiple healthcare providers with isolated patient data.

Provider View Tabs:
  • Provider Details: Hospital/clinic information, contact, address
  • Allotted Devices: Devices assigned to this provider
  • Patient Management: All patients under this provider
  • Primary Ordering Provider Management: POP assignments
Provider Stats:
  • Total Patients count
  • Patients Enrolled (active monitoring)
  • Weekly change indicators
Patient List Filters:
  • Primary Ordering Provider dropdown
  • Patient Enrollment Status (All, Enrolled, Pending, Inactive)
  • Device Status (All, Active, Inactive, Unassigned)
  • Search by Patient ID or Name

3.4 Patient Health Statistics (Quick Stats Panel)

Real-time patient health data display with instant stats and historical trends.

Patient Header:
  • Patient Name with Enrollment Status badge (Enrolled, Pending)
  • Patient ID, Gender, Age
  • Primary Ordering Provider name
  • Current Vitals: Blood Pressure (systolic/diastolic mmHg), Heart Rate (BPM)
  • Latest reading timestamp
  • Last active indicator
Patient Detail Tabs:
  • Results: Vitals readings with alert indicators
  • Notes: Clinical notes and observations
  • Device: Assigned device information
  • Patient Profile: Demographics, contact info
  • Medical Profile: Conditions, medications, allergies
  • Patient Care Team: External care providers
  • Activity: Patient interaction history
Alert Notifications:
  • Inline alerts: "Patient's last reading is 128/92 mmHg. This indicates high blood pressure. Further evaluation is advised."
Vitals Graphs (Time-Series):
  • Blood Pressure results graph (weekly view) - Systolic/Diastolic lines
  • Heart Rate result graph (weekly view) - Pulse line
  • Date range selector (This Week, This Month, Custom)

3.5 External Care Provider Directory

Manage patient's external care team including primary care physicians and specialists.

Care Provider Types:
  • Primary Care Provider (PCP)
  • Specialist 1, Specialist 2, etc.
Provider Information:
  • Name, Email, Specialty (Dentist, Nutritionist, Dietician, etc.)
  • NPI Number (National Provider Identifier)
Access Management:
  • Assign New provider to patient
  • Share access (grant view permissions)
  • Revoke access (remove permissions)
  • View Profile, Remove from care team

3.6 Bulk Patient Onboarding

Efficient enrollment of multiple patients via CSV upload.

Steps to Follow:
  1. Download PDC spreadsheet template (CSV format)
  2. Fill all Patient Information with respective POP ID
  3. Make sure to save changes locally
  4. Save the file in CSV format
  5. Upload the CSV file via drag-and-drop or file browser
  6. Click Submit button to process
UI Elements:
  • Download Template button
  • File upload area (drag-and-drop supported)
  • Submit button
  • Validation feedback for errors

3.7 PDC Billing Module

Automated tracking for RPM billing eligibility and CPT code determination.

Billing Information Screen:
  • Eligibility Status: Eligible (green) / Not Eligible indicator
  • Month Selector: View billing data by month (e.g., Nov 2023)
  • POP Associated: Primary Ordering Provider name
  • Calls Taken: Number of clinical calls/interactions
Per-Device Billing Data:
  • Device Label: BPM Device 1, BPM Device 2, etc.
  • Device ID: Unique identifier
  • Device Status: Active/Inactive
  • Readings Received: Count of readings for billing period
CPT Code Logic:
  • 99453: Initial device setup and patient education (one-time)
  • 99454: Device supply with daily recording (16+ days/month required)
  • 99457: First 20 minutes of clinical time per month
  • 99458: Each additional 20 minutes of clinical time

4. Key User Flows

4.1 Patient Enrollment Flow

Step Actor Action System Response
1 Staff Click "Enroll New Patient" Display enrollment form
2 Staff Enter patient demographics, diagnosis codes Validate required fields
3 Staff Capture digital consent signature Store consent with timestamp
4 Staff Scan device barcode or enter serial Associate device with patient
5 Staff Generate app installation QR code Display QR code for patient
6 Patient Scan QR, install app, pair device BLE pairing wizard guides patient
7 Patient Take first reading Reading syncs, enrollment complete

4.2 Daily Clinical Review Flow

Step Actor Action System Response
1 Nurse Log in to dashboard Show patient list with alert badges
2 Nurse Click "Alerts" filter Show only patients with active alerts
3 Nurse Select critical alert patient Display patient detail with readings
4 Nurse Review readings history and trends Show chart and data table
5 Nurse Add clinical note, mark alert reviewed Log time spent, save note, clear alert
6 Nurse Optionally escalate or call patient Log escalation, initiate call

7. Technical Considerations

Platform Requirements

  • Web App: React-based SPA, responsive design, modern browsers (Chrome, Safari, Edge)
  • iOS App: Swift/SwiftUI, iOS 14+, CoreBluetooth for BLE
  • Android App: Kotlin, Android 10+, BLE support
  • Backend: Cloud-hosted API, PostgreSQL database, HIPAA-compliant infrastructure

Integration Points

  • BLE medical device protocols (standard health profiles)
  • Push notification services (APNs, FCM)
  • Email service for alerts and reports
  • Future: EHR integration via HL7 FHIR APIs

Security & Compliance

  • HIPAA compliance: encryption, access controls, audit logs, BAAs
  • SOC 2 Type II certification for hosting
  • Role-based access control (RBAC)
  • Data retention per healthcare regulations
